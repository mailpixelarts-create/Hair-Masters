PROJECT 10 — PRODUCTION IMAGE PROMPT BIBLE
THE HAIR RITUALIST
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Hair Ritualist, Hair Styling World 10.

This is not a generic “spa haircare” prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

scalp care, treatment ritual, hair health restoration, sensory luxury, slow beauty, botanical intelligence, touch-led craft, and a deeply intentional treatment world built around nourishment, wellness, softness, and repair rather than cut architecture, fashion editorial styling, or red-carpet glamour.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a stylist whose work sits at the intersection of hair health, scalp ritual, treatment craft, and luxury restorative care
a hair artist known for scalp massage, oil rituals, steam treatments, restorative masks, healthy hair transformation, sensory experience, and a slower, more intimate relationship with beauty
a portfolio world where healing, touch, atmosphere, and hair vitality are the hero
a craft-led visual system built around care, softness, ritual, repair, and elevated wellness intelligence
2. PROJECT WORLD SUMMARY — THE HAIR RITUALIST
Core world

The Hair Ritualist is the restorative care, scalp ritual, and treatment-intelligence world inside the Hair Styling Worlds system.

This stylist is not selling “a hair spa.”
This stylist is selling hair wellness direction, scalp-first care, treatment ceremony, restorative luxury, ingredient intelligence, healthy-hair transformation, and an emotionally immersive ritual experience.

The work should feel connected to:

luxury scalp facials and treatment rooms

intimate scalp massage and steam rituals
healthy shine restoration, hydration repair, and softness recovery
elevated salon wellness photography

a slower, almost meditative approach to hair beauty
The imagery must communicate
restoration

emotional exhale

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models who can convincingly inhabit luxury treatment, scalp ritual, and restorative beauty contexts.

Use a cast that can support multiple ritual-care narratives:

6. GLOBAL HAIR RULES
Hair styling direction across the world

The Hair Ritualist is about healthy hair, ritual touch, and scalp-first beauty.

Use a mix of:

glossy healthy long hair with natural movement
damp hair during treatment application
post-treatment smoothness and shine
softly diffused natural texture after restorative care
scalp-revealing sectioning for treatment imagery
towel-wrapped or robe-context ritual states where appropriate
braid / section / oil-application setups for process imagery
hair that looks touchable, hydrated, and alive
before/after improvement in shine, softness, and manageability
refined natural results rather than heavy styling
Hair should never feel
over-curled or glam-styled
red-carpet polished
stiff with hairspray
editorially “dirty” in a way that undermines the care world
product-greasy unless specifically in an oil-application moment
generic salon blowout advertising
cluttered with decorative accessories
disconnected from scalp health or treatment context

Before approving any generated image for The Hair Ritualist, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Does the hair feel healthier, calmer, and more cared for rather than glam-styled?
Can you clearly read restoration, softness, hydration, or scalp-care intent?
Is the image genuinely about ritual and treatment rather than generic beauty photography?
Does the shine look nourished instead of greasy?
Would a serious scalp-care / treatment specialist recognize this as premium restorative work?
Model / styling fit
Does the model feel grounded and believable in a treatment environment?
Is the wardrobe supporting the wellness world rather than distracting from it?
Is the makeup restrained enough that the care story remains central?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read moisture, softness, and healthy hair texture?
Are scalp details premium and clean rather than clinical?
Is the image detailed enough for close crops?
Does the lighting feel restorative and luxurious rather than stock-spa commercial?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like a blowout portfolio instead of a ritual-care world
like a generic day-spa ad
cluttered, low-budget, or wellness-cliché
medically clinical rather than luxurious
overly greasy or badly lit
emotionally empty or commercially bland
too glam, too bridal, or too fashion-styled for a restorative brand world
22. BUILD COMMANDMENT

When generating imagery for The Hair Ritualist, remember:

This world is not about “spa hair.”

It is about restorative intimacy and scalp-first luxury care.

The image must feel like the work of a stylist who understands:

hair health as beauty
scalp ritual as premium experience
touch as craft
nourishment as visual language
wellness without cliché
how to create emotional trust through softness, calm, and restoration

If the image looks like a generic shampoo ad, bridal robe content, influencer self-care content, or stock spa photography, it is wrong for this world.
