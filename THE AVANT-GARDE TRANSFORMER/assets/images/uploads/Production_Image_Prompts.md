PROJECT 07 — PRODUCTION IMAGE PROMPT BIBLE
THE AVANT-GARDE TRANSFORMER
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Avant-Garde Transformer, Hair Styling World 07.

This is not a generic “editorial fashion hair” prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

transformational hair artistry, experimental silhouette, conceptual form-building, shape mutation, material play, fashion-image disruption, and high-concept beauty direction built around hair as an object, a sculpture, a provocation, and a performance medium.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a hair artist who works between fashion, performance, sculpture, and editorial experimentation
a stylist whose job is to transform identity through radical hair direction rather than deliver “wearable salon looks”
a portfolio world where hair can become structure, gesture, armor, abstraction, architecture, distortion, and theatre
a high-fashion image system rooted in shape invention and visual risk
a stylist whose work treats hair as transformative material, not just style
2. PROJECT WORLD SUMMARY — THE AVANT-GARDE TRANSFORMER
Core world

The Avant-Garde Transformer is the experimental, conceptual, shape-mutating world inside the Hair Styling Worlds system.

This stylist is not selling beauty maintenance.
This stylist is selling transformation, concept execution, silhouette invention, fashion-world disruption, sculptural hair construction, and image-making that turns hair into a radical design language.

The work should feel connected to:

avant-garde fashion editorials
conceptual beauty campaigns
sculptural hair installations
hair as headpiece / hair as object / hair as extension of garment
extreme silhouette and negative-space experimentation
shape distortion and asymmetry
wet, lacquered, braided, wrapped, wired, inflated, or directional surface treatment
performance beauty and runway-art hybrids
futuristic editorial portraiture
transformation as identity theater
The imagery must communicate
originality
visual courage
transformation
concept rigor
silhouette invention
fashion authority
technical build intelligence
material experimentation
editorial strangeness with control
uncompromising authorship

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models who can hold difficult shapes, strong beauty compositions, and fashion-world severity.

Recommended cast mix

Use a cast that can carry transformation across multiple visual registers:

2 Indian models for sculptural beauty, strong bone structure, and conceptual fashion portraits
1 Black model for shape, braid, texture, or crown experimentation with strong presence
1 East Asian model for graphic minimalism, sleek futuristic transformation, or silhouette severity

9. GLOBAL LIGHTING RULES
Lighting philosophy

Lighting must reveal:

silhouette edges
surface treatment
construction depth
shine vs matte contrast
structure and negative space
facial geometry in relation to the hair build
material intelligence
Preferred lighting setups
1. Hard-soft hybrid sculptural beauty light

Use for most hero and gallery images.

enough softness for skin
enough edge definition for silhouette and material
2. Narrow side light / rim architecture light

Use for severe shapes, profiles, and negative-space forms.

should carve the silhouette from the background
3. Controlled gloss light

Use for lacquered, wet, patent, metallic, or sculpted surface looks.

must feel editorial, not oily
4. Dark studio spotlight logic

Use sparingly for museum-like or performance-driven concepts.

Avoid
flat catalog lighting
generic ring light beauty setups
muddy shadows hiding the shape
blown highlights on lacquered hair
soft romantic light unless intentionally contrasted against an aggressive silhouette
random club lighting

20. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Avant-Garde Transformer, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Does the hairstyle feel invented, intentional, and structurally credible?
Is the silhouette genuinely transformative, or is it just “weird styling”?
Can you clearly read the shape, material logic, and editorial purpose?
Does the image feel fashion-grade rather than costume-grade?
Does the hair transform the identity of the model in a meaningful way?
Model / styling fit
Does the model feel strong enough to carry the severity of the concept?
Is the wardrobe supporting the transformation rather than distracting from it?
Is the makeup integrated into the concept rather than behaving like generic beauty styling?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read silhouette edges, surface treatment, and construction detail?
Are dark tones retaining information without going muddy?
Is the image detailed enough for close crops?
Does the lighting feel premium and editorial rather than cheap or gimmicky?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like cosplay or costume fantasy
like fashion-school chaos with no composition
structurally weak or fake
too commercial and wearable with no conceptual risk
cluttered, low-budget, or salon-coded
gimmicky without beauty intelligence
emotionally empty despite visual extremity
21. BUILD COMMANDMENT

When generating imagery for The Avant-Garde Transformer, remember:

This world is not about “creative hairstyles.”

It is about hair as transformation, sculpture, and conceptual force.

The image must feel like the work of a stylist who understands:

silhouette engineering
fashion-world transformation
material experimentation
conceptual beauty direction
build logic beneath spectacle
how to mutate identity through hair without losing aesthetic control

If the image looks like a costume wig, a generic edgy salon editorial, or a fantasy concept with no real fashion rigor, it is wrong for this world.
