PROJECT 05 — PRODUCTION IMAGE PROMPT BIBLE
THE COLOR FUTURIST
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Color Futurist, Hair Styling World 05.

This is not a generic balayage or salon-color prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

editorial color design, futuristic pigment work, tonal engineering, transformation through color, precision placement, and fashion-led chromatic identity.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a luxury hair color artist with editorial taste
a pigment laboratory for fashion hair
a color specialist portfolio built around tone, contrast, and placement
a high-end bleach / tone / vivid / neutral transformation studio
a stylist whose work is about hair color as visual authorship, not just maintenance
2. PROJECT WORLD SUMMARY — THE COLOR FUTURIST
Core world

The Color Futurist is the hair-color specialist world inside the Hair Styling Worlds system.

This stylist is not selling “highlights” in a generic salon sense.
This stylist is selling color intelligence, tonal precision, creative transformation, bleach-and-tone mastery, premium glossing, placement strategy, and editorial color storytelling.

The work should feel connected to:

fashion color campaigns
bleach transformations

neutral blonding done at a luxury level
copper, platinum, espresso, ash, mushroom, and futuristic vivid work
placement design
root shadow control
dimension through ribbons, blocks, panels, veils, and money pieces

color as identity architecture
The imagery must communicate
color authority

transformation through pigment

modernity
precision and taste
controlled experimentation
hair as color sculpture

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models who can carry strong color work and whose faces do not compete with the hair.

Recommended cast mix

Use a recurring cast with diversity in skin tone and hair base:

2 Indian models for brunette, copper, espresso, gloss, and creative tone work
1 fair-skinned model suited to platinum / ash / editorial blonding imagery
1 Black model for rich brunette, copper, vivid panel, or color placement work on textured / blown-out hair
1 East Asian model for precision bob color, cool-tone gloss, or futuristic block color work
1 androgynous model for experimental fashion color imagery
Casting requirements

Models should feel:

editorial
fashion-literate
not influencer-coded
capable of severe beauty portraiture
believable for luxury color transformations
visually strong enough for close tone / placement detail crops
Avoid
overly commercial “salon after” smiles
beauty-influencer posing
weak hair density that makes color work unreadable
generic stock-model energy
models whose makeup overpowers the color story
6. GLOBAL HAIR RULES
Hair styling direction across the world

The Color Futurist is about color first, but the styling still needs to support it.

Use a mix of:

sleek straight finishes to show placement and shine
soft controlled bends to reveal dimension
polished blunt bobs for graphic color
glossy long lengths for tonal gradients
sculpted waves only when they reveal ribbons / highlights / panels
wet-look editorial finishes for select futuristic images
controlled texture where it helps reveal depth and contrast
strong center parts or precise side parts where shape supports the color
Hair should never feel
over-curled into generic bridal glamour
frizzy to the point color disappears
fried, broken, or visibly overprocessed unless part of a “before” image
cheap influencer “fresh balayage selfie” content
casual or lazy in finish
overloaded with accessories
random rainbow novelty unless the prompt specifically calls for futuristic vivid work

9. GLOBAL LIGHTING RULES
Lighting philosophy

Lighting must reveal:

tonal separation
shine and gloss
root depth
placement detail
healthy surface texture
dimension in highlights / lowlights / panels
the relationship between face, hair, and color field
Preferred lighting setups
1. Sculptural editorial beauty light

Use for hero and most gallery portraits.

directional but flattering
reveals tone without flattening shine
2. Controlled gloss light

Use for platinum, gloss brunette, copper, and detail shots.

narrow highlight lines to show surface finish
still preserve natural texture
3. Soft futuristic rim + key combination

Use for selected experimental or vivid images.

adds color-drama without obscuring the actual pigment result
4. Neutral atelier daylight / salon light hybrid

Use for process imagery.

believable technical environment
not ugly fluorescent green
Avoid
flat ring-light salon portraits
overexposed blondes with no tonal detail
muddy dark hair where gloss disappears
hard flash that makes hair look dry
oversaturated colored lighting that distorts the actual hair color
10. GLOBAL CAMERA / FRAMING RULES
Camera language

This project should feel photographed by someone who understands hair color as a precision visual discipline.

Preferred focal feel
85mm / 105mm for hero and beauty portraits
50mm for editorial upper-body color portraits
35–50mm for salon atelier / process / environment
100mm macro for gloss, placement, root melt, and tonal detail
Composition language

Use a mix of:

Hero framing
upper-torso editorial beauty portraits
off-center compositions with space for headline copy
direct gaze, calm profile, or 3/4 head turn that reveals the color work
Gallery framing
front portraits showing money pieces / face framing
side and back views for tonal ribbons and placement
bob portraits for graphic color
long hair portraits for dimensional waves and gloss
creative vivid portraits for futuristic edge
Detail framing
root melt close-ups
money piece and front hairline detail
gloss surface macro
bob edge and color line detail
foil / section / tone close-ups where relevant
Process framing
brush + bowl mixing
bleach / foil application
toner painting
rinsing / glossing
final section check under atelier lighting

21. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Color Futurist, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Is the color result the main visual event?
Is the tone believable, expensive, and intentional?
Does the hair still look healthy enough to support luxury color work?
Is the placement visible and readable in the image?
Does the result feel like authored pigment work rather than a generic salon service?
Model / styling fit
Does the model feel editorial enough to support fashion-color imagery?
Is the wardrobe subordinate to the color story?
Is the makeup clean enough that it doesn’t compete with the hair?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read gloss, tone, and dimension?
Are blondes retaining depth rather than blowing out?
Are brunettes / coppers / vivids retaining richness without fake oversaturation?
Is the image detailed enough for close crop usage?
Transformation-specific checks

For before/after pairs:

Is the framing matched properly?
Is the wardrobe unchanged?
Is the background consistent?
Does the “before” remain premium rather than embarrassing?
Is the “after” clearly elevated through color intelligence and finish?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like a generic balayage Instagram ad
like a low-end salon transformation post
too bridal or glam-beauty driven
over-neon or gimmicky
damaged and untrustworthy
weak in gloss, tone clarity, or placement visibility
too commercial and smiley

This world is not about “getting your highlights done.”

It is about color authorship.

The image must feel like the work of a stylist who understands:

pigment behavior
bleach integrity
tonal refinement
placement design
gloss as luxury
transformation through controlled chemistry
editorial hair color as image-making

If the image looks like a generic salon balayage post, a social-media transformation reel thumbnail, or a color result where the tone feels cheap or unconsidered, it is wrong for this world.
