PROJECT 06 — PRODUCTION IMAGE PROMPT BIBLE
THE EDITORIAL MINIMALIST
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Editorial Minimalist, Hair Styling World 06.

This is not a generic “clean girl hair” prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

precision simplicity, quiet luxury hair direction, shape discipline, refined restraint, immaculate finish, fashion-editorial understatement, and high-end minimalism where every cut, line, and texture decision matters.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a luxury editorial stylist known for immaculate restraint
a modern salon artist whose work is about line, finish, and balance
a quiet-luxury hair portfolio built around control rather than spectacle
a fashion beauty world where simplicity is executed at a very high level
a stylist whose work is about clarity, proportion, silhouette, and polished understatement
2. PROJECT WORLD SUMMARY — THE EDITORIAL MINIMALIST
Core world

The Editorial Minimalist is the quiet luxury / precision simplicity world inside the Hair Styling Worlds system.

This stylist is not selling dramatic transformations, hyper-volume glam, or color spectacle.
This stylist is selling shape intelligence, cut refinement, line purity, finish control, soft polish, subtle movement, and modern editorial restraint.

The work should feel connected to:

clean fashion portraiture
immaculate bobs and long line cuts
glass-hair precision
luxury blowouts with restraint
soft bends used only to reveal silhouette
tucked hair, controlled flyaways, and beautiful necklines
minimalist beauty campaigns
neutral palettes and architectural styling
expensive simplicity
“nothing loud, everything resolved”
The imagery must communicate
discipline
taste
precision
restraint
expensive finish
quiet confidence
cut quality
balance and proportion
editorial calm
modern luxury without excess

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models who can carry severe simplicity.
Faces, posture, and hair must all work in a more restrained image language.

Recommended cast mix

Use a coherent cast with variation in face shape, skin tone, and hair type:

2 Indian models for glossy brunette, blunt cuts, center-part polish, and luxury blowout imagery
1 East Asian model for graphic bob, precision line, and sleek editorial portraiture
1 Black model for straightened editorial finish, sculpted minimal silhouette, or polished natural texture restraint
1 fair-skinned model for pale neutral beauty campaigns and severe minimal portraits
1 androgynous or angular-featured model for fashion-forward minimalist cut imagery
Casting requirements

Models should feel:

editorial

not influencer-coded
believable in luxury fashion / magazine beauty contexts
Avoid
overtly commercial smile-heavy casting

“bridal pretty” softness as the main read

20. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Editorial Minimalist, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Does the hair feel resolved, controlled, and expensive?
Is the simplicity actually precise, or does it just look plain?
Are the cut line, finish, and silhouette readable?
Does the hair look healthy and editorial rather than commercial?
Does the image elevate restraint into luxury?
Model / styling fit
Does the model feel fashion-literate enough to carry a quiet image?
Is the wardrobe supporting the silhouette rather than competing with it?
Is the makeup clean enough to stay subordinate to the hair?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read gloss, line, and surface?
Are darker hair tones retaining depth and dimension?
Is the image detailed enough for close crops?
Does the lighting feel soft and premium rather than flat?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like a generic salon blowout ad
too bridal or glam
too influencer-coded
messy without intention
weak in finish quality
loud in styling or color
emotionally generic rather than editorial and composed
21. BUILD COMMANDMENT

When generating imagery for The Editorial Minimalist, remember:

This world is not about doing “simple hair.”

It is about precision restraint.

The image must feel like the work of a stylist who understands:

cut line discipline
finish control
quiet luxury
shape proportion
surface gloss
editorial understatement
the difference between plain and precise

If the image looks like a generic blow-dry bar campaign, a bridal-prep beauty shoot, or a social-media “clean girl” hair post with no authorship, it is wrong for this world.
