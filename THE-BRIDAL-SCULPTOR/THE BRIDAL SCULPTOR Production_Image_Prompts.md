# 2. WORLD SUMMARY — THE BRIDAL SCULPTOR

## Core world

The Bridal Sculptor is the **ceremonial luxury bridal hair world**.

This is not "wedding hair" in the generic vendor sense.

This world is built around the idea that bridal styling is a highly sensitive form of image-making where hair must solve for:

* beauty
* longevity
* emotion
* ceremony
* photography
* intimacy
* elegance
* movement
* dress compatibility
* veil / accessory integration
* identity recognition
* pressure management

The stylist is positioned as someone who can sculpt a bride's presence for one of the most photographed and emotionally loaded days of her life.

## The image system must communicate

* bridal hair as sculpture
* emotional intelligence
* elegance with endurance
* harmony with the bride
* timeless image memory
* softness with structure
* ceremony-ready longevity
* camera-perfect from every angle
* veil and accessory integration
* luxury bridal atelier energy

# 4. GLOBAL CASTING RULES

## Model casting direction

The Bridal Sculptor should feel like a diverse, elevated bridal portfolio — not a one-face or one-ethnicity portfolio.

## Recommended casting mix for Project 03

* **2 South Asian / Indian models** — luxury bridal, strong bone structure
* **1 Middle Eastern model** — sculptural features, veil-compatible styling
* **1 Caucasian / fair-skinned model** — classic bridal, soft romantic energy
* **1 deeper-skinned model** — rich tones, luminous skin, elegant presence
* **1 East Asian model** — clean lines, modern bridal minimalism

## Casting requirements

Models should feel:

* real brides, not runway models
* emotionally composed, not overly smiley
* luminous skin, natural beauty
* bone structure strong enough for beauty crops
* hair suitable for sculptural updo / half-up / wave work
* modern, elegant, expensive
* calm, confident, held

## Avoid

* influencer-looking faces
* overly commercial stock-photo smiles
* beauty pageant styling
* generic "bridal model" energy
* heavy expression acting
* overly retouched to the point of losing humanity

# 5. GLOBAL HAIR RULES

## Hair outcomes across the project should prioritize

* sculpted low chignons with softness
* romantic half-up structures with face framing
* polished waves with hold and movement
* veil-integrated ceremony styling
* modern bridal updos with texture
* clean bridal knots with elegance
* reception transition hair
* timeless up-structures that photograph beautifully
* visible pin work and structural integrity
* soft tendrils and nape details

## Hair should never look

* stiff or over-sprayed
* crunchy
* overcurled
* dry
* over-teased
* pageant-like
* generic "bridal bun"
* obviously AI-generated
* like a Pinterest wedding board
* fragile or falling apart

# 6. GLOBAL MAKEUP + WARDROBE RULES

## Makeup

Makeup should support the hair and never dominate.

Use:

* luminous skin
* restrained complexion work
* soft sculpting
* neutral lip or muted rose tones
* brushed brows
* subtle eye definition
* clean editorial bridal finish
* natural, elevated, "heightened herself"

Avoid:

* glitter
* dramatic lashes
* bridal sparkle
* Instagram glam contour
* heavy lipstick focus
* beauty looks that pull attention from the hair

## Wardrobe

Wardrobe should be minimal, premium, and subordinate to hair.

Use:

* silk robes (ivory, champagne, blush)
* clean gown necklines (strapless, off-shoulder, V-neck)
* minimal jewelry — studs, delicate chains
* exposed collarbone / neck / hair line
* dressing-room intimacy energy

Avoid:

* loud prints
* sequins
* chunky jewelry
* visible logos
* trend-heavy fashion styling
* anything that competes with the hair

# 7. GLOBAL LOCATION / SET RULES

## Primary set language for The Bridal Sculptor

Use one of these controlled environments:

### A. Luxury bridal suite

* soft morning window light
* ivory / cream / champagne palette
* silk curtains, upholstered surfaces
* clean vanity or mirror area
* quiet, intimate, held energy
* elevated but believable

### B. Couture bridal atelier

* clean editorial set
* neutral backdrop with soft texture
* minimal props — chair, mirror, veil stand
* ideal for hero, detail, and gallery portraits

### C. Getting-ready sanctuary

* hotel suite or private dressing room
* soft robes, bouquet details, veil laid out
* window light as primary source
* intimate, emotional, calm

### D. Quiet editorial atmosphere vignette

* hair accessories, veil, pins, combs
* silk fabric, soft surfaces
* jewelry details, rings, earrings
* bridal morning still-life

## Avoid environments that feel

* cheap hotel room
* cluttered bridal shop
* generic wedding venue dressing room
* influencer vanity setup
* brightly colored lifestyle interiors
* random home bathroom
* suburban salon décor
* chaotic bridesmaid-room energy

# 8. GLOBAL LIGHTING RULES

## Lighting for The Bridal Sculptor must show

* softness
* luminosity
* dimension
* texture
* movement
* emotional warmth
* camera-ready finish

## Preferred lighting styles

### 1. Luxury bridal soft directional light

Use for hero portraits and bridal portraits.

* large soft source from side/front-side
* controlled shadow
* strong shine on sculpted hair
* skin remains soft but not flat
* warm, luminous, flattering

### 2. Window light intimacy

Use for getting-ready and atmosphere imagery.

* natural-feeling soft window light
* gentle shadow fall
* romantic, intimate, calm
* morning bridal energy

### 3. Editorial beauty fill

Use for gallery and detail shots.

* clean, even beauty lighting
* enough contrast to read hair texture
* flattering without being flat
* premium editorial feel

### 4. Veil and accessory highlight

Use for veil-integrated and detail imagery.

* subtle back edge or rim to catch veil texture
* emphasize hair accessory placement
* soft sparkle without glitter

## Avoid lighting that creates

* blown highlights on hair
* muddy color
* flat overhead salon light
* obvious ring-light reflections
* hard beauty light that destroys softness
* yellow tungsten contamination
* cold clinical feel

# 9. GLOBAL CAMERA / FRAMING RULES

## Camera language

The Bridal Sculptor should feel like it was shot for a luxury bridal editorial with sculptural hair intelligence.

## Preferred focal language

* **85mm / 105mm portrait lens feel** for hero and bridal portraits
* **50mm editorial portrait feel** for mid-shots and movement portraits
* **100mm macro / beauty close-up feel** for detail shots
* **35–50mm documentary beauty feel** for process and atmosphere shots

## Framing system

Use a controlled mix of:

### Hero framing

* vertical editorial bridal portrait
* horizontal hero crop with negative space for website headline
* 3/4 portrait with visible hair sculpting and emotional composure
* veil-integrated hero composition

### Gallery framing

* front 3/4
* back 3/4 (hair showcase)
* side profile (sculptural silhouette)
* seated bridal portrait with hair drape
* standing shoulder crop with movement
* full look context with dress and bridal mood

### Detail framing

* nape-to-midlength crop
* pin work close-up
* veil placement detail
* tendrils and face-framing texture
* accessory and comb placement
* crown and parting sculptural detail

### Process framing

* hands pinning
* stylist finishing
* veil placement
* mirror review moment
* trial session intimacy

# 10. GLOBAL COLOR / FILM / GRADING RULES

## Grading direction

The Bridal Sculptor should feel **warm, luminous, ivory-toned, and expensive** — not trendy or hyper-saturated.

## Color grading goals

* warm ivory skin tones
* soft champagne hair highlights
* creamy bridal palette preservation
* warm-neutral luxury tonality
* preserved shadow detail
* soft luminous highlights without clipping
* no fake HDR sharpness
* romantic but not over-sugared

## Finish references

* luxury bridal editorial digital capture
* medium-format fine-art wedding feel
* polished but not plastic retouching
* optional ultra-subtle film grain in atmosphere images only
* hair shine must look photographic, not CGI

## Avoid

* orange skin
* grey dead skin
* oversaturated blush
* cold blue bridal tone
* crunchy over-sharpening
* over-smoothed hair strands
* waxy skin retouching
* overly filtered Pinterest aesthetic

# 21. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Bridal Sculptor, check:

## Hair quality

* Does the hair look sculpted but soft?
* Is the structure visible and intentional?
* Are the ends clean and healthy?
* Is the style camera-perfect from the angle shown?
* Does it feel bridal without being generic?

## Styling quality

* Is the makeup subordinate to the hair?
* Is the wardrobe minimal and premium?
* Does the model feel like a luxury bride, not an influencer?
* Does the overall image feel emotionally composed?

## Lighting quality

* Can you clearly read the sculptural form?
* Are highlights controlled and luminous?
* Is there enough contrast without killing softness?
* Does the lighting feel warm and inviting?

## World fit

* Does the image feel like bridal sculpture?
* Does it belong in a luxury bridal atelier?
* Would it sit naturally inside The Bridal Sculptor site without breaking the world?
* Does it communicate trust, softness, and elegance?

## Rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

* too generic bridal
* too influencer
* too commercial-stock
* too "Pinterest wedding board"
* too synthetic / AI-plastic
* too overstyled
* too stiff or over-sprayed
* too empty of visible sculptural value
