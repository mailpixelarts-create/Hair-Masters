THE RED CARPET DIRECTOR
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Red Carpet Director, Hair Styling World 08.

This is not a generic “glam hair” prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

luxury event hair direction, celebrity-level finish, polished glamour, camera-proof styling, red carpet silhouette control, formal hair architecture, high-end beauty execution, and event-image storytelling built around hair that performs under flash, movement, scrutiny, and fashion pressure.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a stylist who operates at the level of premiere nights, award shows, couture events, luxury campaigns, and public-facing beauty moments
a hair artist whose job is to create flawless, high-stakes glamour that reads from every angle and under every camera condition
a portfolio world where hair must balance polish, personality, structure, movement, and event-ready authority
a stylist whose work turns formal beauty into direction, strategy, and image control, not just “pretty hair”

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models who can convincingly inhabit celebrity, event, couture, and formal-glam contexts.

Use a cast that can support multiple red-carpet hair languages:

20. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Red Carpet Director, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Does the hairstyle feel camera-ready, intentional, and luxurious?
Is the finish genuinely red-carpet level, or does it drift into bridal / prom / influencer glam?
Can you clearly read silhouette, polish, and event-worthiness?
Does the image feel like it could sit in a celebrity beauty portfolio?
Does the hair communicate control under flash, movement, and scrutiny?
Model / styling fit
Does the model feel expensive enough to carry a couture-event image world?
Is the wardrobe supporting the glamour rather than distracting from it?
Is the makeup formal, polished, and camera-aware rather than pageant-heavy?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read gloss, hairline finish, and silhouette edge?
Are dark tones retaining depth without becoming muddy?
Is the image detailed enough for close crops?
Does the lighting feel premium and celebrity-grade rather than cheap or influencer-coded?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like bridal catalog filler
like prom or pageant glamour
like a generic salon updo ad
cluttered, low-budget, or event-cheap
over-hairsprayed and stiff
weak at the hairline or silhouette edge
emotionally generic instead of poised and strategic
