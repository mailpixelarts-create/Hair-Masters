PROJECT 06 — PRODUCTION IMAGE PROMPT BIBLE
THE TEXTURE POET
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Texture Poet, Hair Styling World 06.

This is not a generic “curly hair inspo” prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

texture-led hair artistry, shape intelligence, softness with intention, curl architecture, coily volume, wave pattern poetry, tactile beauty, and emotionally rich editorial hair storytelling built around natural movement and form.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a high-end texture specialist who understands curls, coils, waves, shrinkage, density, and shape
a stylist whose work is about honoring natural pattern rather than flattening it into generic beauty imagery
a premium hair portfolio built around texture as authorship, not trend
a fashion-aware salon artist who can turn curl craft into editorial storytelling
a stylist whose work is about sculpting living texture, not just “before and after curly hair results”
2. PROJECT WORLD SUMMARY — THE TEXTURE POET
Core world

The Texture Poet is the texture, curl, coil, and movement world inside the Hair Styling Worlds system.

This stylist is not selling “curly hair services” as a salon menu category.
This stylist is selling texture understanding, curl architecture, shape refinement, hydration intelligence, density control, silhouette design, and editorial celebration of natural movement.

The work should feel connected to:

luxury curl portraiture

expertise with texture

emotional richness

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models whose texture reads beautifully on camera and whose hair can carry real silhouette variation.

Recommended cast mix

Use a strong texture-led cast across wave, curl, coil, and afro categories:

2 Black models with distinct textures—one with tight coils / afro volume, one with defined curls or textured updo potential
1 Indian model with strong wave / curl pattern for soft texture and premium curl-shape imagery
1 mixed-race or Afro-Latina model with dense curl volume and editorial beauty presence
1 model with short natural texture / cropped coils for shape and silhouette variation
1 model with long spiral curls for movement-heavy and ritual-driven imagery
Casting requirements

Models should feel:

emotionally expressive without overperforming
fashion-literate
comfortable in close beauty work
believable in premium texture-care contexts
able to carry softness, stillness, and movement equally well

must preserve texture dimension

2. Side-skimming texture light

Use for afro shapes, coily volume, and close detail work.

3. Warm daylight

Use for ritual and interior portraits.

should feel intimate and believable

harsh flash
ring-light tutorial aesthetics
muddy underexposure that loses definition
blown highlights on shiny curls
over-contrasty lighting that makes texture feel dry

20. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Texture Poet, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Does the texture feel alive, healthy, and authored?
Is the shape intentional, or does it just look uncontrolled?
Can you clearly read pattern, density, and silhouette?
Does the hair feel touchable rather than stiff?
Does the image elevate texture into luxury craft and editorial beauty?
Model / styling fit
Does the model feel fashion-literate enough to carry the emotional texture world?
Is the wardrobe supporting the silhouette rather than competing with it?
Is the makeup subordinate to the texture and skin story?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read curl pattern, hydration, and silhouette edge?
Are darker textures retaining depth and separation?
Is the image detailed enough for close crops?
Does the lighting feel soft and premium rather than tutorial-like or flat?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like a generic curly-hair tutorial thumbnail
like a salon product ad with no authorship
too dry, crunchy, or unhealthy
too glam-curled instead of naturally textured
cluttered or commercially noisy
weak in silhouette and shape intelligence
emotionally generic instead of tactile and soulful
