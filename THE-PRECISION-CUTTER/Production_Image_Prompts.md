PROJECT 09 — PRODUCTION IMAGE PROMPT BIBLE
THE PRECISION CUTTER
1. PURPOSE OF THIS FILE

This document defines the production image prompt system for The Precision Cutter, Hair Styling World 09.

This is not a generic “short hair editorial” prompt pack.

This file is for building the actual visual system of the website around the real identity of the project:

cut architecture, line discipline, geometric control, exact proportion, silhouette refinement, dry-cut intelligence, edge precision, and high-end haircut image-making built around shape, section, tension, and structure rather than generic beauty styling.

The prompts below are written to create imagery that feels like it belongs to:

a stylist whose primary language is cut design
a hair artist known for bobs, blunt lines, internal structure, perimeter discipline, shape engineering, and tailored silhouette refinement
a portfolio world where haircut shape is the hero, not curl styling, event glamour, or decorative ornament
a craft-led visual system built around precision, control, proportion, and the intelligence of the cut itself

The Precision Cutter is the architecture-of-the-haircut world inside the Hair Styling Worlds system.

This stylist is not selling “a nice haircut.”

5. GLOBAL CASTING RULES
Casting direction

This project needs models with faces, necks, jawlines, and posture that can support cut-focused portraiture.

Recommended cast mix

Use a cast that can support multiple precision-cut languages:

2 Indian models for strong jawline-framing bobs, collarbone lobs, blunt fringes, sleek mid-length cut portraits, and contemporary luxury cut imagery
1 East Asian model for graphic bob, precision fringe, and severe line portraiture
1 Black model for precision natural cut, sculpted short cut, or exact textured shape where line discipline still reads clearly
1 androgynous model for severe crop / precision short cut / identity-neutral cut architecture
1 fair-skinned editorial model for pale minimal beauty portraits where shape and perimeter are highly visible
Casting requirements

Models should feel:

editorial rather than commercial
physically composed and posture-aware
able to hold stillness in a way that supports line and symmetry
believable in a luxury haircut portfolio rather than a generic beauty campaign
strong enough to carry very minimal styling without the image collapsing
Avoid
influencer beauty faces
generic smiling salon-model casting
weak neck / jawline presentation
heavy glamour makeup overwhelming the cut
hyper-romantic posing
hair that obscures the actual shape of the cut unless the concept is specifically about fringe or face-framing

9. GLOBAL LIGHTING RULES
Lighting philosophy

Lighting must reveal:

perimeter lines
cut edges
fringe density and shape
nape precision
weight lines and silhouette
smoothness without losing texture intelligence

Preferred lighting setups
1. Soft directional editorial light

Use for most hero and gallery portraits.

enough softness for skin
enough direction to reveal shape and line
2. Side light for silhouette definition

Use for bobs, pixies, profile cuts, and nape detail.

should carve the outer perimeter clearly
3. Clean overhead-soft plus frontal fill

Use for top-line and fringe visibility where symmetry matters.

4. Minimal high-contrast studio light

Use sparingly for severe modernist portraits.

Avoid
muddy light that hides the cut line

ring-light beauty setups
overly soft dreamy haze
harsh unflattering flash unless conceptually intentional
blown highlights on dark polished hair

21. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

Before approving any generated image for The Precision Cutter, verify the following.

Hair / concept fit
Does the haircut feel cut-led, exact, and structurally intentional?
Can you clearly read the line, perimeter, weight, and silhouette?
Is the image genuinely about haircut architecture rather than styling tricks?
Does the shape look expensive because of the cut, not because of glamour styling?
Would a serious haircut specialist recognize this as disciplined cut work?
Model / styling fit
Does the model have the stillness and facial structure to support the cut?
Is the wardrobe helping frame the haircut rather than distracting from it?
Is the makeup restrained enough that the haircut remains the hero?
Lighting / image quality
Can you clearly read fringe edges, nape line, and perimeter?
Are dark cuts retaining edge definition?
Is the image detailed enough for close crops?
Does the lighting feel premium and editorial rather than generic salon content?
World-fit rejection triggers

Reject the image if it feels:

like a styling portfolio instead of a cut portfolio
over-curled or glam-styled to hide the haircut
like generic salon advertising
cluttered, low-budget, or tutorial-coded
weak at the perimeter, nape, or fringe
emotionally empty or commercially bland

When generating imagery for The Precision Cutter, remember:

This world is not about “nice hair.”

It is about shape discipline through cutting.

The image must feel like the work of a stylist who understands:

perimeter control
geometry
weight lines
balance and proportion
cut architecture over styling decoration
how to create visual authority through exactness
